# Resource: Reading List & Book Notes

A curated reading list focused on **asymmetric returns** — ideas that compound.

---

## Reading Queue (prioritized)

### Tier 1 — Read First (foundational, high-leverage)
1. **The Almanack of Naval Ravikant** — Eric Jorgenson — Wealth, happiness, leverage
2. **Atomic Habits** — James Clear — Systems > goals, habit architecture
3. **The Psychology of Money** — Morgan Housel — Behavioral finance, compounding
4. **Zero to One** — Peter Thiel — Contrarian thinking, monopoly vs competition
5. **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman — Cognitive biases, System 1 vs 2

### Tier 2 — Deep Dives
6. **Antifragile** — Nassim Taleb — Thriving in disorder, optionality
7. **Poor Charlie's Almanack** — Charlie Munger — Mental models, multidisciplinary thinking
8. **Inspired** — Marty Cagan — Product management, discovery vs delivery
9. **Why We Sleep** — Matthew Walker — Sleep science, performance optimization
10. **On Photography** — Susan Sontag — Philosophy of seeing and capturing

### Tier 3 — Expand
11. **The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz — Resilience, leadership
12. **AI Superpowers** — Kai-Fu Lee — AI landscape, US vs China
13. **The War of Art** — Steven Pressfield — Resistance, creative discipline
14. **Steal Like an Artist** — Austin Kleon — Creativity as remixing
15. **Outlive** — Peter Attia — Longevity science, healthspan

### Tier 4 — When Ready
16. **Sapiens** — Yuval Noah Harari — Human history, big picture
17. **The Beginning of Infinity** — David Deutsch — Knowledge, progress, possibility
18. **The Art of Thinking Clearly** — Rolf Dobelli — Decision-making errors
19. **Seeking Wisdom** — Peter Bevelin — Munger + Buffett thinking compiled
20. **Competing in the Age of AI** — Iansiti & Lakhani — AI strategy for business
